# Procter-Gamble
This report analyzes Procter & Gamble’s FY 2025 annual performance, covering financial highlights, industry positioning, segment revenues, geographic distribution, balance sheet and income statement analysis, auditor’s opinion, and strategic outlook, emphasizing innovation, shareholder value, sustainability, and leadership transition.

##  Project Overview
This project reviews **Procter & Gamble’s FY 2025 Annual Report**, focusing on financial performance, governance, industry context, and strategic initiatives. It includes balance sheet and income statement analysis, auditor’s reports, and management commentary.

##  Key Highlights
- **Company Profile:** Founded 1837, HQ Cincinnati, Ohio; operates in FMCG sector with brands like Tide, Pampers, Gillette, Oral-B, Olay.
- **Financials FY 2025:**
  - Net Sales: $84.3B (+0.3%)
  - Net Earnings: $16.0B (+1.1%)
  - Dividend per Share: $4.08
  - Market Cap: ≈ $370B
- **Revenue Breakdown:**
  - Fabric & Home Care: 36%
  - Baby/Feminine/Family Care: 24%
  - Beauty: 18%
  - Health Care: 14%
  - Grooming: 8%
- **Geographic Sales:** North America 52%, Europe 22%, Greater China 7%, Latin America 7%, Asia Pacific 7%, IMEA 5%.
- **Liquidity & Leverage:**
  - Current Ratio: 0.70 (below industry avg. 1.0)
  - Debt-to-Assets: 58% (slightly above industry avg. 50%)
- **Auditor’s Opinion:** Deloitte & Touche LLP issued a clean (unqualified) opinion, confirming compliance with GAAP.
- **Leadership Transition:** Shailesh Jejurikar to become CEO in Jan 2026, succeeding Jon Moeller.
- **Sustainability:** Strong commitments to climate, water, waste reduction, and DEI initiatives.

##  Assignments Covered
1. **Obtaining the Annual Report** – Source and process.
2. **Company Information** – Industry, products, global reach.
3. **Annual Report Overview** – Chairman’s letter, management discussion, auditor’s report.
4. **Balance Sheet Analysis** – Common size, ratios, liquidity, leverage.
5. **Income Statement Analysis** – Vertical format, margins, profitability, trends.

##  Conclusion
P&G demonstrates **financial resilience, innovation, and shareholder focus**, with strong global positioning in FMCG. Despite slightly weaker liquidity compared to peers, its profitability, equity strength, and sustainability initiatives reinforce long-term stability and investor confidence.
